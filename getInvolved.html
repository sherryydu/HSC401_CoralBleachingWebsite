Help at home

You don't have to travel to the reef to help out, you can do your part in your own neighbourhood! Hover over any of the following boxes to learn more!

Use less fertilizer

Using less fertilizer in your lawn or gardens means there's a lower chance of fertilizer washing into any nearby oceans.


Choose reef-safe sunscreens

Oxybenzone can cause bleaching. Reef-safe sunscreens don't have any traces of oxybenzone, which makes them safe when swimming at the beach.

Support sustainable fishing

As a consumer, you have the power to put your money where your mouth is. To support fisheries that don't use destructive methods, look for a blue MSC label.

Skip driving alone

To decrease your carbon footprint, opt for the bus, a bike, or a simple walk to and from places. You can also carpool too! The possibilities are endless!

Pick energy efficient lightbulbs

Being energy efficient is not only good for coral reefs, but it's nice on your wallet too! You can reduce your carbon footprint (and your energy bills!).

Use less water

Remember, run-off of chemicals like fertilizers happen because of one thing: water. Using less water can prevent the likelihood of run-off into nearby oceans.

Keep shorelines clean

Corals hate trash. Wherever you go, make sure to clean up after yourself. Trash can wash into the rivers, end up in the ocean, and eventually travel to reefs.

Choose your pet fish wisely

Tropical salt fish are cool pets, but sometimes they can be sourced in ways that kill corals. Be sure to pick ethically sourced aquarium fish.
